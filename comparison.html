Fan Chart

FamilySearch / MyHeritage

You at the center. Ancestors radiate outward in concentric arcs. Each ring is a generation. No lines — position alone shows relationship.

  • Fits many generations in one screen
  • No scrolling needed
  • Color-coding by status/county is powerful
  • Hard to read names at outer rings
  • No room for dates/notes on cards
  • Doesn't show research status clearly

My Recommendation

For your app, I'd combine Ancestry's recursive centering with MyHeritage's detail panel. The horizontal pedigree is the clearest way to show direct ancestors and research status (confirmed/probable/brick wall). The detail panel is where you'd surface document purchase suggestions — "Buy Joseph Mooney + Alice Lynch's marriage cert (£2) to unlock 4 grandparents." That's your app's core value proposition, and it needs to be visible within the tree context.

A fan chart could be a secondary "overview" view for seeing the whole tree at once, but the horizontal pedigree is your primary research interface.
